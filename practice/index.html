Philly Cheesesteak Sliders

If you’re looking for a game-day treat that’ll leave everyone talking, these cheesesteak sliders are the way to go. Packed with tender steak, caramelized onions, melty cheese, and all the right seasonings, they’re perfect for your Super Bowl spread. Let me walk you through every step so you can serve up sliders that will steal the show and leave none to go!

Making the Steak

Let’s start with the star of the show—the steak! You’ll want thinly shaved steak to get that perfect cheesesteak texture. You can typically find thinly shaved ribeye steak at most grocery stores, either pre-sliced in the meat section or at the deli counter. Ribeye, sirloin, or flank steak are great options for this, and if you can’t find it pre-sliced, don’t worry—you can easily make it at home!

How to Make Thinly Shaved Steak at Home:

If your store doesn’t carry thinly shaved steak, it’s easy to make your own. Here’s what you need to do:

  1. Freeze the Steak: Place your steak in the freezer for about 30-45 minutes. This firms it up, making it much easier to slice thinly.

  2. Slice Against the Grain: After the steak firms up, remove it from the freezer. Using a sharp knife, slice the steak thinly against the grain (those lines running through the meat). Aim for slices about 1/8 inch thick. If you have a mandoline slicer, you can use that for even thinner slices!

Once your steak is ready, heat some butter and oil in a pan over medium-high heat, and toss the steak in once the pan is nice and hot. Season it with salt, paprika, mustard powder, and black pepper—this will give the steak all the flavor it needs. As the steak cooks, use a spatula to break it apart if necessary and ensure it gets evenly browned. Once the steak is fully cooked, add the Worcestershire sauce, which will give the steak a rich, savory flavor. Next, deglaze the pan with some beef broth, scraping up all the delicious brown bits that have stuck to the bottom. Let the broth simmer for a couple of minutes to reduce and thicken into a flavorful sauce. Set the steak aside while you prep the veggies.

Variation Tip: If you like mushrooms, this is the perfect time to add some! Slice up some cremini or button mushrooms and toss them in with the steak. The mushrooms will absorb all that delicious broth and steak flavor, adding an extra layer of savory goodness to your sliders.
